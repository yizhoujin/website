I am an economist. I study how data and AI transform market failures, focusing on information asymmetry and imperfect competition in insurance and digital platforms. My work uses economic theory, large administrative datasets, structural econometrics, and field experiments.

Before academia, I worked in technology (Twitter, Lyft, Ant Group/Alibaba), finance (Citi, JPMorgan, Houlihan Lokey), and policy (RBA and IVASS). I co-created Twitter Communities and Luohan Academy, designed telematics programs for usage-based auto insurance pricing and accident prevention, led an e-commerce training program for over two million new sellers, and executed tech IPOs and M&As (Palo Alto Networks, Guidewire, GrubHub/Seamless, Permira/Genesys).

I am Assistant Professor of Strategy and Economics at the University of Toronto. Previously, I was Gilbert Center Fellow at UC Berkeley and received my PhD from Harvard under Ariel Pakes and BA from Berkeley under David Romer. I am a 北京四中 alum.
